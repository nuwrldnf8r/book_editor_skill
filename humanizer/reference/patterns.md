# General-prose AI-tell catalog (lookup reference)

These are the surface and structural tells that apply across registers. The
SKILL.md handles the book- and course-specific ones; this file is the full
backstop, condensed from Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing." Consult it — don't
let it turn the edit back into a checklist. Always work voice-first.

## Vocabulary / phrasing
- **AI vocabulary** (overused post-2023): delve, tapestry, testament,
  underscore, showcase, pivotal, intricate, interplay, vibrant, boasts,
  nestled, groundbreaking, realm, landscape, navigate, elevate, unlock, embark,
  harness, seamless, robust, leverage, myriad, plethora, foster, holistic,
  synergy, empower, transformative, resonate, captivating, meticulous,
  profound, moreover, furthermore. Flag clusters, not singletons.
- **Filler phrases:** "in order to" → "to"; "due to the fact that" → "because";
  "at this point in time" → "now"; "has the ability to" → "can"; "it is
  important to note that" → (delete).
- **Excessive hedging:** "could potentially possibly" → "may".
- **Persuasive-authority tropes:** "the real question is," "at its core," "in
  reality," "what really matters," "fundamentally," "the heart of the matter."
- **Aphorism formulas:** "X is the language of Y," "X becomes a trap," "the
  currency of," "the architecture of." Replace with the concrete claim.

## Structure / rhetoric
- **Significance inflation:** "marking a pivotal moment," "a testament to," "in
  today's ever-evolving landscape," "stands as," "underscores its importance."
- **Superficial -ing padding:** trailing participle phrases that fake depth —
  "...reflecting the community's deep connection," "...highlighting its role."
- **Copula avoidance:** "serves as / stands as / boasts / features" instead of
  plain "is / are / has."
- **Negative parallelism:** "It's not just X, it's Y"; "not merely... but...";
  tailing negations like "no guessing," "no wasted motion."
- **Rule of three / tricolon:** forced groups of three for false completeness.
- **False ranges:** "from X to Y" where X and Y aren't on one scale.
- **Synonym cycling (elegant variation):** protagonist → main character →
  central figure → hero, all for one person.
- **Vague attribution:** "experts argue," "observers have noted," "industry
  reports," with nobody named.
- **Signposting:** "let's dive in," "here's what you need to know," "without
  further ado."
- **Generic upbeat conclusions:** "the future looks bright," "exciting times lie
  ahead," "a step in the right direction."
- **Knowledge-cutoff / gap-filling:** "as of my last update," "while specific
  details are limited," "maintains a low profile," "likely grew up." Say what
  isn't known or cut it; don't dress a guess as fact.

## Surface (caught by the checker)
- Em dashes (—), en dashes (–), double-hyphen dashes (--).
- Curly quotes and apostrophes.
- Emoji decoration on headings/bullets.
- Mechanical boldface runs; inline-header bullet lists ("**Performance:** ...").
- Title Case In Headings (use sentence case).

## Chatbot residue (when text was pasted from a chat)
- "Great question!", "Certainly!", "I hope this helps", "Would you like me to...",
  "Let me know if...", "You're absolutely right."

## What is NOT a tell on its own
Polish, formal vocabulary, a single em dash, curly quotes from an autocorrecting
editor, one short emphatic sentence, plain/dry prose, common transition words in
isolation, unsourced claims. Flag clusters, not isolated hits.
