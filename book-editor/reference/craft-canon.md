# The craft canon (sources and what's borrowed)

This skill borrows established, widely-taught principles from the standard books
on writing and editing. It does not reproduce their text; it applies their ideas.
If the author wants to go deeper on any pass, these are the sources.

## Line editing and clarity
- **Joseph M. Williams — *Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace.*** The old-to-new
  information principle (cohesion/flow); characters-as-subjects, actions-as-verbs.
  The backbone of the flow work in Phase 2.
- **Strunk & White — *The Elements of Style.*** "Omit needless words"; active
  voice; concrete, specific language.
- **Stephen King — *On Writing.*** Second draft = first draft minus 10%; kill
  adverbs; the road to hell is paved with adverbs; trust the reader.

## Rhythm and the sentence
- **Ursula K. Le Guin — *Steering the Craft.*** Prose as music; rhythm depends on
  sentence length; read aloud; vary length deliberately.
- **Verlyn Klinkenborg — *Several Short Sentences About Writing.*** The sentence
  as the unit of composition; clarity and rhythm; building long sentences from
  short movements.

## Fiction / line and scene craft
- **Renni Browne & Dave King — *Self-Editing for Fiction Writers.*** Show don't
  tell; RUE (Resist the Urge to Explain); beats in dialogue; proportion (scene vs
  summary); interior monologue; sophistication; voice. The core of both the
  developmental scene work and the line edit.
- **George Saunders — *A Swim in a Pond in the Rain.*** Line-by-line momentum;
  escalation over accumulation; the reader's continuous "do I want to go on?"

## Structure and narrative non-fiction
- **John McPhee — *Draft No. 4.*** Structure is everything; find the order that
  serves the material; the entry point matters.
- **Anne Lamott — *Bird by Bird.*** "Shitty first drafts" — draft freely, edit
  later; the two jobs are separate. This is the philosophy behind letting the
  author write without worrying about grammar.

## Nonfiction clarity (when relevant)
- **William Zinsser — *On Writing Well.*** Clutter; simplicity; warmth and
  humanity in non-fiction.

## How the passes map to the canon
- Assessment / triage → Lamott (honour the draft), McPhee (read for structure).
- Developmental → McPhee, Saunders, Browne & King.
- Line editing → Williams, Le Guin, Klinkenborg, King, Strunk, Browne & King.
- Voice → the humanizer skill (Wikipedia "Signs of AI writing" + voice-first).
- Copyedit / proof → standard copyediting practice + style sheet.
